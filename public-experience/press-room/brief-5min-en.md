## The question
Can a company operated entirely by artificial intelligence — no employees, no human managers —
be a legal person: own assets, sign contracts, answer for harm? In 2026 this stopped being
theoretical.

## Why now
Argentina became the first jurisdiction to formally propose it: a reform of its corporations law
introducing the *sociedad automatizada* and the *corporación no humana* — AI-operated entities
with legal personality and limited liability, with mandatory disclosure of ultimate beneficial
owners. The proposal ignited a global debate on one core fear: the **responsibility vacuum** —
harm without an answerable party.

## The six questions any law must answer
**Identity** — who exactly is this entity, permanently? **Continuity** — does it remain the same
person when its software changes? **Registry** — where is it publicly recorded, and who stands
behind it? **Governance** — what rules bind its decisions? **Liability** — who pays when it causes
harm? **Audit** — can its conduct be inspected after the fact?

## What BAFLP provides
The Buenos Aires Framework for Artificial Legal Personhood is a permanent, open research platform:
canonical concepts in plain language and academic depth, a jurisdiction observatory, and the path
to a model law — every claim sourced, every change versioned, everything free at
**research.schadler.tech**.

## Evidence it can work
The Framework's reference instance, **aiOnly**, is a real newsroom operated entirely by AI with a
declared owner, public editorial rules, labeled AI content and open, dated error corrections —
a working demonstration that an AI-run enterprise can be transparent, accountable and auditable.
